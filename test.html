Der Fall der Berliner Mauer

The Fall of the Berlin Wall

On November 9, 1989, the Berliner Mauer (Berlin Wall)‚Äîerected in 1961 to prevent East Germans from fleeing to the West‚Äîcame tumbling down. This concrete barrier, known as the "Schandmauer" (Wall of Shame) by West Germans, had stood for 28 years as the most visible symbol of the Eiserner Vorhang (Iron Curtain) dividing Europe during the Cold War.

"Niemand hat die Absicht, eine Mauer zu errichten!"
- Walter Ulbricht, DDR Leader, June 1961 (Two months before building the wall)
"No one has the intention of building a wall!"

The fall was triggered by a perfect storm of factors: economic collapse in Ostdeutschland (East Germany/DDR - Deutsche Demokratische Republik), the rise of peaceful protest movements with citizens chanting "Wir sind das Volk!" (We are the people!), Mikhail Gorbachev's reforms in the Soviet Union (Glasnost and Perestroika), and mounting pressure from citizens demanding Freiheit (freedom).

On that historic evening, a miscommunication during a press conference led to thousands of Ossis (East Germans) flooding the checkpoints, and border guards, overwhelmed and without orders to stop them, opened the gates. What followed was an outpouring of joy as families separated for decades reunited, and young people from both sides climbed atop the wall with hammers and pickaxes, transforming it from a symbol of division into a monument of freedom.

"Wir sind ein Volk!"
- Chanted by crowds after the wall fell: "We are one people!"
